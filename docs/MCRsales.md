Executive summary

Build a Hermes multi-agent outbound growth system for MCR Business Intelligence LLC that identifies, researches, scores, nurtures, and escalates qualified B2B opportunities for MCRcore's managed IT, cybersecurity, network monitoring, ERP support, virtual server, automation, and fractional CIO services.

The system must be tuned to MCRcore's real market position:
– 3–200 employee companies
– strongest fit in manufacturing, supply chain, freight and logistics, insurance agencies, and growth-oriented SMBs
– strongest regional fit for full managed IT, ERP, and systems work in the Midwest and Northern Florida
– North America-wide remote fit for break/fix, network, security, virtual servers, and related remote-delivery services
– buyer targets include CEO/owner, operations leaders, CFO/controller, and IT managers

The system must prioritize quality, relevance, legal compliance, and deliverability over high-volume blasting.

Compliance and deliverability requirements

All commercial email workflows must comply with CAN-SPAM. The FTC states CAN-SPAM applies to B2B commercial email, requires accurate header information, non-deceptive subject lines, a valid physical postal address, a working opt-out mechanism, and honoring opt-outs within 10 business days.

For UK business-to-business marketing, the ICO says marketing to corporate subscribers can differ from marketing to sole traders and partnerships, but marketers still must not conceal identity and must provide a valid address for opt-out requests.

If MCRcore sends to Gmail recipients, Google requires authentication for all senders and SPF, DKIM, and DMARC for bulk senders, with stronger enforcement on non-compliant traffic continuing after November 2025.

Required compliance controls:
– accurate From, Reply-To, and routing information
– non-deceptive subject lines
– valid physical postal address in commercial email
– working opt-out process
– suppression list enforcement
– opt-out honored within 10 business days
– no continued nurture after opt-out
– sender authentication verification
– bounce and spam complaint monitoring

Core functional requirements

10.1 Lead acquisition

The system must:
– add at least 50 net-new qualified leads per day
– search only approved or approved-by-policy sources
– deduplicate against existing database
– assign source provenance to each lead
– tag each lead with candidate service-fit flags

10.2 Lead enrichment

The system must:
– research every new lead before first outreach
– create a concise factual summary
– identify likely operational and IT pain points
– detect known ERP/system clues
– estimate support complexity
– identify likely buyer role relevance
– store confidence level for every inferred field

10.3 Lead scoring

The system must:
– produce fit score, need score, engagement score, and margin band
– prioritize Midwest and Northern Florida for full managed offers
– prioritize North America more broadly for remote-deliverable services
– favor companies showing ERP dependence, growth strain, or compliance burden
– favor companies with 3–200 employees
– rank the top 5 highest-probability unprocessed leads each day

10.4 Outreach generation

The system must:
– create personalized first-touch and follow-up emails
– use the lead's industry, geography, title, and likely pain points
– use the most relevant MCRcore differentiators
– prefer audit-first CTA for cold outbound
– vary message angle across follow-ups
– avoid generic MSP language

10.5 Reply classification

The system must classify replies into:
– likely order / strong sales opportunity
– interested but not ready
– neutral / needs clarification
– not interested
– opt-out
– junk / spam / auto-response / bounce

10.6 Escalation

When a reply shows likely buying intent, the system must:
– generate a sales-ready summary
– include all prior communications
– include recommended package fit
– include estimated value and margin band
– email the summary to the MCRcore sales inbox
– mark the lead as escalated

10.7 Nurture

For interested but unconverted leads:
– send a distinct follow-up at day 7
– send another distinct follow-up at day 30
– send another distinct follow-up at day 90
– re-run reply classification on every new reply
– suppress further sends if lead opts out or becomes invalid

Scoring model tuning

15.1 Fit score

Weight heavily:
– 3–200 employee fit
– target-industry fit
– target-geo fit
– title fit
– ERP/system evidence
– managed-service suitability

15.2 Need score

Weight heavily:
– support complexity
– compliance burden
– remote or hybrid need
– uptime sensitivity
– operational growth strain
– weak internal IT capability

15.3 Offer-fit score

Weight heavily:
– whether MCRcore has a clear package match
– whether the lead is a better audit-first candidate
– whether the lead has wedge-offer potential through cybersecurity or monitoring

15.4 Engagement score

Weight:
– open
– click
– reply
– positive language
– request for price
– request for audit
– referral to another stakeholder

15.5 Margin priority

Use as secondary ranking factor:
– Total Plan and strong monitoring fits score well
– Essential Cybersecurity can be used as wedge, not necessarily a top-margin target
– hourly work remains tentative until pricing is finalized

Messaging requirements

All messages must:
– sound specific and informed
– avoid generic MSP boilerplate
– reference concrete business context
– tie value to uptime, growth, security, operational stability, or system support
– map to likely buyer concerns by title
– use the audit-first motion when appropriate
– remain materially different across nurture stages

Title-aware message emphasis:
– CEO / owner: growth, risk reduction, leverage, no need for full-time internal overhead
– operations leader: uptime, process friction, ERP reliability, issue resolution speed
– CFO / controller: predictability, reduced downtime cost, right-sized spend, compliance risk
– IT manager: co-managed support, specialist depth, coverage, escalation relief

Daily operating workflow

Daily sequence:
load source allowlist and suppression lists
acquire leads until 50 new qualified leads are added
enrich new leads
detect ERP, industry, compliance, and growth signals
map lead to likely service package
score and rank leads
process all inbound replies
move strong responders to high-probability queue
select top 5 highest-probability unprocessed leads
generate and send personalized outreach or follow-up
escalate likely opportunities to MCRcore sales email
schedule future nurture
write KPI and exception summary

Acceptance criteria

The system is accepted when:
– it adds at least 50 approved-source qualified leads per day
– each lead receives a research summary, package recommendation, and score
– top 5 unprocessed high-probability leads are selected daily
– messages are package-aware and ICP-aware
– reply classification routes leads correctly
– likely opportunities are escalated with thread context
– opt-outs suppress future sends
– sender compliance checks occur before every outbound send
– daily KPI output is available
